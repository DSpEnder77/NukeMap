How We Calculated This

Operating Capacity

Sourced from the EIA v2 API (operating-generator-capacity endpoint), filtered to nuclear technology and operating status. Data reflects the most recent monthly snapshot. Units: nameplate capacity in MW.

Retirements

Based on NRC license expiration dates for each operating reactor unit. We do not assume license renewals unless already formally approved by the NRC. If a license has expired but the plant is still operating under a renewal application, we use the requested new expiration date where known.

A reactor retiring in year X means its full nameplate capacity is subtracted from the running total in that year. We use the license expiration date, not a shutdown date forecast.

New Build

Based on projects with active NRC review, DOE ARDP funding, or confirmed construction starts. "Confirmed" means the project is under construction or holds a construction permit or license. "Speculative" means the developer has announced intent and engaged with regulators, but no construction license exists.

Projects are credited in the year their developer has publicly targeted for commercial operation. These dates frequently slip.

The Gap

Net capacity by year = operating base today − cumulative retiring capacity + cumulative new build. The amber area on the chart represents the difference between the current operating baseline and projected net capacity in each future year.

What This Is Not

This is a snapshot, not a forecast. It does not account for: unannounced license renewal applications, policy changes, grid demand shifts, or projects cancelled after this data was compiled. License renewals, if approved, would shift retirement curves to the right. Project cancellations would widen the gap further.
