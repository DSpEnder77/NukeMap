# ADR-0004 — Editorial stance: show, don't tell

**Status:** Accepted · **Era:** V1

## Context
The site makes a case — that firm clean power, nuclear chief among it, matters and is being
let go too cheaply. But a site that *reads* as advocacy is dismissed and never cited. The
persuasion has to survive a hostile reader.

## Decision
**Present, never exhort.** No calls to action, no "we believe," no movement branding. The
argument is carried by honest *selection, sequence, and presentation* of true things — and
by honest comparison, including the parts that cut against the thesis (Banqiao deaths, Vogtle
cost overruns, contested accident tolls). Every individual element must survive a hostile
fact-check; the reader is left to conclude.

The full positioning and the reason this discipline exists is in **`docs/VISION.md` (internal)**.

## Consequences
- **Credibility is the product.** The site reads as a reference work, which is what gets it
  cited — and a citation is worth more than a slogan.
- We deliberately publish facts that weaken the easy version of the argument; that honesty is
  the moat ([0006](0006-provenance-system.md) makes it literal).
- Anti-goals (never do): petitions, donate buttons, dunking on renewables, doom-counting.
